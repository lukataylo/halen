Help that never leaves your Mac

Read on

Every other writing tool asks you to leave. Open a new tab. Paste a draft. Wait while someone else's server thinks for you, then copy the answer back across the wire.

Halen doesn't. It lives wherever you type — Mail, Slack, the message you've been rewriting in Notes for the third time. The model sits on disk. The text never moves.

Type ;polite and a hostile sentence softens. Type ;sig and your signature lands at the caret. Every snippet is a small file you can read, fork, or rewrite tonight.

It's a writing partner that belongs to you. Not a service to log into. Not a subscription to renew. A local agent that runs when you launch it, and stops when you quit.
